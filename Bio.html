A Short Bio

In 2006, I moved to the US and studied at Green River Community College, Auburn, Washington   . At first, I was interested in Food Science but because Biology requires memorizing a lot, I decided to study General Engineering. Two years later, I subsequently transferred to University of Texas at Austin, majored in Petroleum Engineering and finished my BSc. in 2011  . Following after graduation, I went to work on researches at Gulf Coast Carbon Center at Bureau of Economic Geology until June 2012  .

After one year without taking classes, but working on real assignments and well water projects with various researchers at the bureau, I realized that my passion was more into freedom, room for creativity, while still using calculations. At that time, I thought about how it was exciting to explore a more uninhibited major outside my Bachelor's degree. I was interested in software engineering since the field was not too rigid and set in everything, while giving you plenty of possibilities to venture out the theoretical guidelines given and experiment more as you go to work.

I studied my Master's degree at  Pacific States University ,   which is located in the busy and interesting Koreatown, Los Angeles, CA.

My interests are applying programming languages, learning and developing applications and software that would be useful and seeking personal growth and happiness from doing so in the process.

Music is also one of favorite things I enjoy and listen to especially interesting music and unusual artists that we encounter every day from different media.
